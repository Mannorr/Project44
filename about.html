About Us

More Than Outreach. A Movement of Hope.

PROJECT44 exists to prove that one act of compassion, multiplied by many hands, can change the trajectory of a child's life — forever.

Who We Are

Born to Birth Hope.

PROJECT44 began with a conviction: that the children most forgotten by the world are never forgotten by God. Founded by Ene Deborah Ohemu, a medical student at Babcock University and lover of Jesus, PROJECT44 was built on the belief that the Gospel is not just a message to be preached — it is a love to be demonstrated.

We are a faith-driven humanitarian foundation operating across Lagos and Abuja, Nigeria. We don't just bring food — we bring presence. We don't just bring medicine — we bring meaning. Our mission goes beyond immediate welfare; it reaches into the soul.

At PROJECT44, holistic care is not a strategy. It is a calling.

Our Vision

A World Built on Hope.

A world where every child, regardless of background, grows up with the assurance that they are loved by God, supported by community, and destined for a life of purpose and hope.

Our Mission

Hands & Heart of Christ.

To be the hands and heart of Christ to children in need, demonstrating God's love through reliable, compassionate, and community-driven humanitarian action centred on the message of the Gospel.

Our Values

What We Stand On.

Four truths that shape every outreach, every decision, and every conversation at PROJECT44.

01

Faith

Everything we do is rooted in the love of Christ. We serve because we have been served.

02

Compassion

We don't show up to perform charity. We show up as neighbours, as family, as people who care.

03

Community

Lasting change is never built alone. We partner with communities to create impact that belongs to them.

04

Integrity

We are accountable to the children we serve, to our partners, and to the God we represent.

Our Programs
